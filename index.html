RESEARCH EXPERIENCE
Undergraduate Biology Thesis Qct. 2022 – Jun. 2023
-Topic: Characterization of Gold Nanoparticles Synthesized from Alga Anabaena 7120 Phycobiliprotein Crude Extract.
-Description: Demonstrates that PBP from cyanobacteria can synthesize gold nanoparticles (AuNPs), with the AuNPs
primarily localized on the thylakoid membranes of cyanobacterial cells, which are adorned with phycobilisomes
assembled from PBP. The AuNPs exhibited diameters ranging from 20-30 nm, with excellent dispersion, nearly
uniform shapes, and the conversion rate came up to 89.9% for gold ions.
-Result: Explained the mechanism of cyanobacteria synthesizing AuNPs and added new functions to PBP.
-Contribution: Culture of cyanobacterial cells; Extraction of PBP; Analysis of AuNPs characteristics; Gel
electrophoresis of PBP; Integration and analysis of all data.
-Achievement: Currently consolidating experimental results in preparation for potential publication.
One Patent Nov. 2021 – Dec. 2022
-Topic: Method for Producing High-Yield and High-Purity Phycobiliprotein Crude Extract.
-Description: Using the glycerol permeation method, the concentration of PBP extracted from Alga Anabaena 7120
reached as high as 318.61 mg/L, accounting for 95.19% of the total soluble protein content. The purity of PBP
consistently exceeded 4 and reached a maximum of 5.108, far surpassing recognized purity standards. It exhibited
robust protective effects on PBP, two weeks after extraction, UV absorption spectra scans revealed overlapping
characteristic peaks, Fourier spectra detected minimal changes in functional groups.
-Result: Provides an efficient, cost-effective, and simple means of extracting PBP from Alga Anabaena 7120.
-Contribution: Culture of cyanobacterial cells; Extraction of PBP; PBP purity and stability analysis; Gel electrophoresis
of PBP; PBP spectral analysis; PBP reducibility determination; Extraction conditions exploration and optimization;
Partial data integration and analysis.
-Achievement: A patent in China.
Undergraduate Research Fund Project Nov. 2021 – July. 2022
-Topic: Efficient Synthesis of Gold Nanoparticles from Cyanobacteria Mutants.
-Description: Cultivated gold-tolerant mutant strains of cyanobacteria—Synechocystis sp. PCC6803 and Synechococcus
sp. PCC7942. These mutant cyanobacteria could convert gold ions into uniformly spherical AuNPs, and they
synthesized 2-6 times more AuNPs compared to their wild-type counterparts. Observed that the mutant strains exhibited
reduced intrinsic fluorescence, and the PBP decreased by 33.96%, which was associated with the increased production
of AuNPs.
-Contribution: Culture and mutagenesis of algal cells; Exploration of mutagenesis conditions; Analysis of molecular
groups in reaction; Analysis of AuNPs characteristics; Determination of AuNPs reducibility and stability; Partial data
integration and analysis.
-Result: Developed an efficient and environmentally friendly biogenic synthesis method using mutant cyanobacteria for
high-yield AuNPs production.
